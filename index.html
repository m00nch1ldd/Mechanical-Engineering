Mechanical Engineering

  1. Vector force, moment, FBD, EOE, supports, constraints, Lami's theorem (3 forces concurrent).
  2. Trusses and frames: perfect/imperfect (redundant/deficient), method of joints / method of sections.
  3. Kinematics: translation, rotation, rectilinear coordinates, curvilinear coordinates: cylindrical polar and spherical, displacement, velocity, acceleration, projectile motion.
  4. Dynamics: Newton's second law (equation of dynamics / D’Alembert’s principle / work–energy formulation / impulse–momentum formulation), spring force, impact/collision: perfectly elastic / semi-elastic / plastic.
  5. Rotation: angular displacement, velocity, acceleration, mass moment of inertia (I), eccentricity.
  6. General plane motion: relative coordinates and measurements.
  7. Centroid (Varignon’s theorem), moment of inertia (second moment of area), product of inertia, polar moment of inertia, parallel and perpendicular axes theorem, tensor nature of MOI, transformation equations, principal moment of inertia and principal axes, radius of gyration.
  8. Axial loading: stress and strain, Hooke’s law, modulus of elasticity, elongation, statically indeterminate problems: compatibility equations, thermal stress and strain.
  9. Torsional loading / twisting: twisting moment at a section, torsional shear stress, stress distribution, Hooke’s law for shear, modulus of rigidity, torsional equation, angle of twist, torsional stiffness, shaft strength (solid vs hollow).
  10. Shear and bending: distributed loading, shear force and bending moment, bending stress, pure bending, neutral surface and axis, bending equation / flexural equation, flexural rigidity, beam strength / moment of resistance, section modulus, shear stress in beams, shear stress distributions in standard cross-sections.

    Slope and deflection: equation of deflection curve / elastic curvature, methods of integration, Macaulay’s method, moment area method.
  11. Combined loading: 2D stress and strain, stress transformation equations, principal stresses, maximum shear stress, graphical stress analysis, Mohr circle.

    2D strain analysis: transformation equations, principal strains and directions, maximum shear strain, strain gauge and rosettes (rectangular / star).

    Combined bending and torsion, generalized Hooke’s law and elastic constraints, Poisson’s ratio, volumetric deformation, hydrostatic and deviatoric stress, bulk modulus of elasticity, relation between constitutive properties, thin shells (circumferential / hoop strain).
  12. Columns and energy methods: buckling, Euler’s theory of columns, effective length, eccentric loading of columns (circular / rectangular cross-section).

    Strain energy (axial, torsion, bending, shear, general), distortion energy / shear strain energy, resilience and toughness, deflection by energy methods, Castigliano’s theorem.
  13. Fundamentals of mechanisms: links, joints / pairs (lower / higher), mechanism, DOF / mobility (Gruebler / Kutzbach formula), redundant DOF, slider-crank chain and mechanism, inversions, four-bar chain, Grashof’s criterion, double slider-crank chain.
  14. Kinematic analysis of planar mechanisms: velocity of reciprocating / rotating links, instantaneous centre (I-centre), relative I-centre, Kennedy’s theorem, graphical method.

    Acceleration: link in complex motion, slider and slotted link, Coriolis acceleration (magnitude and direction), analytical method (loop equations).
  15. Dynamic analysis of planar mechanisms and flywheel: slider-crank mechanism, speed fluctuations, maximum kinetic-energy fluctuation.
  16. Governors: controlling force, isochronism and hunting, gravity-controlled centrifugal governor, Proell governor, spring-controlled centrifugal governors, Hartung governor, Hartnell governor, insensitiveness and coefficient.
  17. Balancing: rotary masses, reciprocating engines, locomotive balancing, in-line engines.
  18. Gears: fundamentals of gearing, types, spur-gear terminology and design parameters, module, velocity ratio, law of gearing, involute profile properties, line of contact, interference and minimum number of teeth, force analysis and design of spur-gear tooth, beam strength criterion.
  19. Gear trains: simple, compound, epicyclic (planetary).
  20. Vibrations: natural frequency, structural vibrations, free / forced / natural vibrations, damped vibrations, SHM, steady state, isolation and transmissibility.
  21. Design for static loading: theories of failure — Rankine, Tresca / Guest, St-Venant, Haigh, Von-Mises (distortion energy theory), generalized theories and graphical comparison.
  22. Design for dynamic loading: reversed beam bending test, stress cycles, fatigue life, reversed/repeated/fluctuating stresses, S-N (strength vs life) curve (log-log plot), stress amplitude, mean stress, endurance strength, ferrous/non-ferrous, estimated and corrected endurance strength-Marin factors, notch sensitivity of material (q), theoretical stress concentration, Theories of dynamic (fatigue) failure: Soderberg criteria, Goodman criteria, Gerber's criteri, ASME Elliptical criteria, Modified Goodman criteria, Combined stresses in Fatigue, cumulative damage in fatigue - Miner's equation.
  23. Design of joints: Riveted joint: head, shank, tail. lap/butt: single/double strap. types based on rows (one, two,...), relative position: chain, zig-zag. Design parameters: width of plate, plate thickness, rivet diameter, hole diameter, pitch, transverse pitch, diagonal pitch, margin, total no of rivets, no of rivets in a row, allowable tensile stress of plate, allowable shear stress of rivet, allowable bearing/crushing stress of rivet. Analysis: tearing strength of plate, shearing strength of joint, crushing/bearing strength of joint, strength of joint, efficiency of joint. Welded joint: butt/fillet(parallel/transverse/circumferential). Design parameters: plate width, thickness, weld length, leg size/size of fillet weld, throat thickness of fillet weld, allowable tensile stress of weld material, allowable shear stress of weld material. Analysis: Butt joint tensile loading, butt joint shear loading, parallel fillet, transverse fillet, torsion of fillet welded joint. Bolted joint: Metric thread(right handed). design parameters: root/core diameter, major/nominal/shank diameter, pitch, root thickness, thread height, pitch diameter, number of threads. standard relations for ISO metric thread, designation of metric threads. pretension/preload of bolted joints. stress in threads. eccentric loading of bolt/rivet joints.
  24. Design of Bearings: rolling contact bearings (rolling element, antifriction bearings): Ball bearings: deep groove - radial and axial load, angular contact - radial+axial load, self-aligning - shaft misalignment. Roller bearings: straight cylindrical - heavy radial load(no axial), tapered roller - radial+axial, spherical roller - shaft misalignment, needle roller - space constraint. subject to dynamic loading - designed for life. \(L_{10}\) (minimum/rating life), \(L_{50}\) (median/average life). Load-life equation, basic dynamic load rating, life in hours, cumulative loading of rolling contact bearings. Sliding contact bearings (sleeve/journal bearings): L,d(short/long/square). Design parameters: journal radius, journal diameter, bearing(sleeve), sleeve diameter, radial clearance, diametral clearance, journal length, allowable bearing pressure, viscosity of lubricant. Analysis: resultant normal/radial force, resultant friction force, coefficient of friction, Petroff's equation, Frictional torque loss, frictional power loss, Sommerfield number.
  25. Design of brakes and clutches: radial- block(internal/external(shoe)) brakes, band brakes, band and block brakes, centrifugal clutches.
